## Take a guess. Just one.

Because you will be sleep deprived when you will upload your v0.9.1_final_revised_working_with_afterthought_accountedFor pcb gerber files to your pcb maker of choice.
And you wont notice you layout is in the wrong direction. And you wont notice that your header rail is 0.2 mm too far to the right. And you expecially wont notice that you placed a via right after one of the header holes which, you know, they act like vias...

But, you found the perfect solution to your packaging problem so you must not sleep thorught it, for it will be long gone when you wake up.
Note that here "long gone" is a good thing, since what we have in our mind at this point is just the idea, whit no thoughtful implementation whatsoever. But let's not this distract us form designign our wrongly crafted missing link.

So you power up your pc, unleash your usb mouse and you're installing Autodesk Eagle, because that's the last software you used to design a pcb and frankly, the only one you (think) you know how to use.
Of course, at this point you will spend at least 1 hour to remember every useful command and the good practices, if you ever even knew some.

Oh but now, now you're on it. That's it. You're up. You can build the thing.

Caliper in one hand, mouse in the other hand, keyboard in whatever body limb you feel most comfortable with. The schematics are all in your head, as clear as a day. The board is perfectly layed out in you frontal cortex. It is there before you're eyes: this 18x21mm piece of mastery which will not even have a single active component on it. You placed the power rails capactor pads just to feel ok with yourself. You know you will not solder anything to those. All this effort just to not use wires. Ah! What a feeling!
